Presentation Board Remake

According to my sources, you should have six TIFF files created from your FormZ work in Studio and the prior half of this semester. Those six lucky images will be featured on your shiny new presentation board layout.

What, again?

Just think how easy it will be to improve on your last presentation board without the burden of completing the actual project. Also think how liberated you will feel by simply working on the design of the presentation board and worrying about generating subject matter.

This project allows you to focus on some applied graphic design techniques, without the burden of generating content.

Project Parts

You will create your 24" x 36" presentation board from the following:

  1. 6 FormZ Rendered Images
  2. Your Project Title
  3. Your Concept Statement

Feel free to scan any material swatches, add extra images of furniture or lighting, etc. The above items are required.

Recomendations.

Avoid Boring.

This is an exercise in page design, typography, and layout. Since you don't have to worry about how this presentation board will affect your studio project grade...be free! Take some design risks. Try something you couldn't do with the hand-built presentation boards you have made in the past.

Do not think of this project as simply re-making your last presentation board in digital form.

Requirements - Due Dates

The envelope please...and the requirements are...

  • Presentation Boards must include all parts listed above. All 6 images must be used.
  • Presentation Boards must be formatted 24" x 36" (ARCH D), landscape or portrait.
  • Presentation Boards must be plotted in color on plotter bond (or higher quality paper if desired).
  • Presentation Boards must be completed by the beginning of class on April 18th

Most work on this project will take place during class, while work outside of class should focus on the Illustrator Challenge.
